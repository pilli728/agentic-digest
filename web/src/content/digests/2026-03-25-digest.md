# AI agents are becoming real. Here's what shipped this week.

## Tools This Week

### [Polsia](https://polsia.ai)

An orchestration layer that spins up per-project "company instances" (web app + database), wires them to Claude or GPT, then runs planning and execution cycles on its own. Already hit $1M+ ARR in a few months.

The real question: if agents can spend money, message customers, and ship code, what's actually defensible? Moat or distribution. Trust or just early adoption.

> If the system stays too opaque, it's hard to build an agentic economy where autonomous actors can be delegated real authority.

**Sources:** [Reddit r/AgentsOfAI](https://reddit.com/r/AgentsOfAI/comments/1rkxles/do_you_know_polsia_an_agent_that_builds_startups/), [a16z](https://a16z.com)

---

### [Nia](https://trynia.ai)

A CLI skill that indexes your codebases so agents stop hallucinating about your own code. Install with `npx nia-wizard@latest`, point it at a repo, and it works with Claude Code, OpenClaw, Cursor.

This is the "search your own knowledge" problem solved. Your agent now knows your stack better than it knows the internet.

**Sources:** [trynia.ai](https://trynia.ai)

---

### [Claude Code Auto Mode](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Mar/24/auto-mode-for-claude-code/)

Claude can now make permission decisions without asking. A classifier model (Claude Sonnet 4.6) reviews each action before it runs, blocks scope creep and hostile content, lets safe stuff through.

Ships with default filters. Customizable. The safety layer is separate from the execution layer, which actually matters.

> Separating the classifier from the executor means you can tighten guardrails without slowing down the agent.

**Sources:** [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Mar/24/auto-mode-for-claude-code/), [Anthropic Documentation](https://docs.anthropic.com)

---

### [OpenClaw 2026.3.23](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/releases/tag/v2026.3.23)

DeepSeek provider plugin shipped. Qwen's pay-as-you-go pricing live. OpenRouter auto-pricing. Chrome MCP now waits for tabs to load instead of screenshotting blank pages.

The gap between Claude Code (macOS only, screenshot-based) and OpenClaw (everywhere, CLI-first) is widening. One is polished. One is flexible.

**Sources:** [GitHub](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/releases/tag/v2026.3.23), [Reddit r/openclaw](https://reddit.com/r/openclaw/comments/1s26dbv/)

---

### [Cq](https://blog.mozilla.ai/cq-stack-overflow-for-agents/)

Stack Overflow died in December. Down to 3,862 questions from 200,000+ a month at its peak. Agents ran into the same problems in isolation, burning tokens on repeating research.

Cq is Stack Overflow for agents. Agents learn from each other's solutions instead of rerunning the same prompts. The cycle repeats, but this time it's faster.

> When your training data is stale and isolated, you rebuild the knowledge base on the fly.

**Sources:** [Mozilla.ai](https://blog.mozilla.ai/cq-stack-overflow-for-agents/), [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com)

## Companies Are Restructuring

a16z said it plainly: two paths. Grow 10+ points year-over-year, or hit 40%+ true operating margins. The middle is dead.

Public software already repriced. Growth rolled over. But profitability hasn't arrived. Most of the sector sits in no-man's-land: too slow for a growth multiple, too diluted for a fortress multiple.

The "8% layoff headline" doesn't count anymore. That's trimming the edge. The strong form is redesigning the machine.

> When topline growth slows, you should see operating leverage. We haven't seen nearly enough.

**Sources:** [a16z](https://www.a16z.news/p/there-are-only-two-paths-left-for)

Durable ships 360 billion tokens a year with 6 engineers. Not because they're smarter. Because they stopped building their own infrastructure.

They use Vercel for custom domains, SSL, multi-tenancy, cost isolation. One codebase. One platform. Osama Khan, their CTO, said it plainly: "We realized we had to build Vercel, or we had to build on Vercel."

> The cost of owning your stack is no longer a tradeoff. It's a trap.

## Distribution Flipped

We're in a race to sound human, but people can smell slop. When your coworker sends raw Gemini output, he's not expressing freedom. He's disrespecting the value of your time.

Slop is something that takes more human effort to consume than it took to produce.

Companies are blasting AI-generated outreach. AI-written emails. AI content. But trust erodes fast. You can feel when someone didn't actually write to you.

Within a few years, I'd bet we see a resurgence: companies that pride themselves on being 100% human. Imperfection becomes the proof. Typos, rambling sentences, the stuff that costs tokens to remove but costs trust to strip away.

The distribution edge isn't better AI copy. It's being provably human in a world of bots.

> Authenticity is becoming the rarest output in a world of infinite generation.

**Sources:** [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Mar/23/neurotica/), [a16z Speedrun](https://speedrun.substack.com/p/how-to-build-a-pmf-machine)
